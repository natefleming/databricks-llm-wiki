# The One Ring

The One Ring, also known as the Ruling Ring, the Master Ring, and Isildur's Bane, is the central artifact in J.R.R. Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings. It was forged by the Dark Lord [[sauron]] in the fires of [[mount-doom]] during the Second Age to control the other Rings of Power and dominate the free peoples of [[middle-earth]].

## Creation

Sauron forged the One Ring in the volcanic fires of Orodruin (Mount Doom) in the land of [[mordor]], approximately in the year 1600 of the Second Age. He poured much of his own power and will into the Ring, making it an extension of himself. The Ring bore an inscription in the Black Speech of Mordor, visible only when heated:

> One Ring to rule them all, One Ring to find them,
> One Ring to bring them all, and in the darkness bind them.

## Powers

The Ring grants several abilities to its bearer:
- **Invisibility**: When worn by mortals, the Ring renders the wearer invisible to those in the physical world, shifting them partly into the wraith-world.
- **Extended life**: The Ring dramatically extends the natural lifespan of its bearer, as seen with [[bilbo-baggins]] and [[gollum]].
- **Corruption**: The Ring exerts a powerful corrupting influence, amplifying the bearer's desire for power and slowly bending their will to [[sauron]]'s purpose.
- **Domination**: Its primary purpose is to control the other [[rings-of-power]], particularly the Three Elven Rings and the Seven Dwarf Rings.

## History

### The Second Age
After its creation, Sauron used the Ring to wage war against the Elves. The Last Alliance of Elves and Men, led by [[gil-galad]] and Elendil, defeated Sauron at the Battle of Dagorlad. [[isildur]] cut the Ring from Sauron's hand, but rather than destroying it, claimed it as his own. The Ring betrayed Isildur at the Gladden Fields, slipping from his finger, and was lost in the River Anduin for over two thousand years.

### Discovery and the Third Age
The Ring was found by [[gollum]] (then called Smeagol) around the year 2463 of the Third Age. He kept it for nearly 500 years in the Misty Mountains until [[bilbo-baggins]] discovered it during the events recounted in The Hobbit. Bilbo kept the Ring for sixty years before passing it to his nephew [[frodo-baggins]] at [[gandalf]]'s urging.

### The Quest of the Ring
[[frodo-baggins]] carried the Ring from [[the-shire]] through [[rivendell]], [[moria]], [[lothlorien]], and ultimately to [[mount-doom]] in [[mordor]], where it was destroyed when [[gollum]] fell into the volcanic fire while clutching it. The destruction of the Ring caused the immediate downfall of [[sauron]] and the collapse of his realm.

## Influence on Bearers

Every bearer of the Ring was affected by its corrupting power:
- **[[sauron]]**: The Ring was an extension of his will and power.
- **[[isildur]]**: Claimed the Ring as weregild and was betrayed by it.
- **[[gollum]]**: Possessed the Ring for ~500 years, becoming twisted and obsessive.
- **[[bilbo-baggins]]**: Kept the Ring for 60 years; it slowed his aging but made parting with it difficult.
- **[[frodo-baggins]]**: Carried the Ring to Mount Doom but was unable to voluntarily destroy it.
- **[[boromir]]**: Attempted to take the Ring from Frodo, driven by desire to use it to save [[gondor]].
