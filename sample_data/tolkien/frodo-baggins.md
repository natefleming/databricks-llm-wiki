# Frodo Baggins

Frodo Baggins is the protagonist of The Lord of the Rings and the Ring-bearer who carried [[the-one-ring]] from [[the-shire]] to [[mount-doom]] in [[mordor]] to destroy it. He is the nephew and adopted heir of [[bilbo-baggins]].

## Early Life

Frodo was born on September 22, 2968 of the Third Age in [[the-shire]]. Orphaned at a young age when his parents drowned in a boating accident on the Brandywine River, he was raised by relatives in Brandy Hall before being adopted by his older cousin [[bilbo-baggins]] at Bag End.

Frodo grew up surrounded by Bilbo's stories of adventure and his unusual collection of maps, books, and Elvish manuscripts. He developed an uncommon curiosity about the wider world, unusual for a Hobbit of [[the-shire]].

## The Ring-bearer's Quest

### Inheritance of the Ring
When [[bilbo-baggins]] departed [[the-shire]] after his eleventy-first birthday party, he left Bag End and all his possessions to Frodo — including [[the-one-ring]]. [[gandalf]] suspected the Ring's true nature but took seventeen years to confirm it, returning to warn Frodo that he must leave [[the-shire]] and bring the Ring to [[rivendell]].

### The Fellowship
At the Council of Elrond in [[rivendell]], Frodo volunteered to carry [[the-one-ring]] to [[mordor]]. The Fellowship of the Ring was formed to protect him: [[gandalf]], Aragorn, [[boromir]], Legolas, Gimli, [[samwise-gamgee]], Merry, and Pippin.

### The Journey
The Fellowship traveled through [[moria]], [[lothlorien]], and down the Anduin River. After [[boromir]]'s attempt to take the Ring and the Fellowship's breaking at Amon Hen, Frodo and [[samwise-gamgee]] continued alone toward [[mordor]].

Guided (and misguided) by [[gollum]], they crossed the Dead Marshes, approached the Black Gate, traversed Ithilien, climbed the stairs of Cirith Ungol, and finally entered [[mordor]].

### Mount Doom
At the Crack of Doom inside [[mount-doom]], Frodo succumbed to the Ring's power and claimed it for himself, unable to voluntarily destroy it. [[gollum]] attacked him, bit off his finger, and fell into the fire with the Ring, inadvertently accomplishing what no one could do by will alone. [[the-one-ring]] was destroyed and [[sauron]] fell.

## After the War

Frodo returned to [[the-shire]] with his companions, but found he could never fully recover from the physical and psychological wounds of bearing the Ring. The wound from the Morgul-blade on Weathertop, the sting of Shelob, and the spiritual burden of the Ring had left permanent damage.

On September 29, 3021 of the Third Age, Frodo departed from the Grey Havens on a ship to the Undying Lands, accompanied by [[gandalf]], [[bilbo-baggins]], Galadriel, and Elrond — granted this grace as a Ring-bearer.

## Character

Frodo's defining quality is his extraordinary resilience and capacity for mercy. Despite being an ordinary Hobbit with no special powers or combat training, he endured the Ring's corruption longer than any other bearer. His decision to spare [[gollum]]'s life — against [[samwise-gamgee]]'s judgment — ultimately proved essential to the Ring's destruction, embodying Tolkien's theme that mercy and pity can change the course of history.
