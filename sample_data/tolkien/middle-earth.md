# Middle-earth

Middle-earth is the primary setting of J.R.R. Tolkien's legendarium, the fictional continent where The Hobbit, The Lord of the Rings, and The Silmarillion take place. It is part of the larger world of Arda, created by the godlike being Iluvatar.

## Geography

Middle-earth is a vast continent with diverse regions:

### Western Lands
- **[[the-shire]]** — The peaceful homeland of the Hobbits, located in the region of Eriador. A pastoral land of rolling green hills, farms, and comfortable hobbit-holes.
- **[[rivendell]]** (Imladris) — The hidden valley refuge of the Elves, ruled by Elrond Half-elven. Located in the foothills of the Misty Mountains.
- **The Grey Havens** (Mithlond) — The westernmost Elven port, from which the Elves depart for the Undying Lands.

### Central Regions
- **[[moria]]** (Khazad-dum) — The greatest of the Dwarf kingdoms, carved deep beneath the Misty Mountains. Abandoned after the Dwarves awakened a Balrog in its depths.
- **[[isengard]]** — The fortress of [[saruman]], featuring the tower of Orthanc and surrounded by a ring wall.
- **[[lothlorien]]** — The enchanted forest realm of the Elves, ruled by Galadriel and Celeborn. A place of extraordinary beauty preserved by the power of one of the [[rings-of-power]].
- **Fangorn Forest** — Ancient forest home to the Ents, the tree-herding giants.

### Eastern and Southern Lands
- **[[gondor]]** — The greatest kingdom of Men, ruled from Minas Tirith. Once the northern kingdom of the Dunedain, it serves as the primary bulwark against [[mordor]].
- **Rohan** — The land of the horse-lords, the Rohirrim. Allied with [[gondor]] and known for their cavalry.
- **[[mordor]]** — The dark land of [[sauron]], surrounded by mountain ranges on three sides. Contains [[mount-doom]] (Orodruin), where [[the-one-ring]] was forged and ultimately destroyed.

## Peoples of Middle-earth

### Elves
The firstborn children of Iluvatar. Immortal, wise, and fair, the Elves are divided into many kindreds. By the Third Age, most Elves are departing Middle-earth for the Undying Lands, weary of the world.

### Men
The secondborn children of Iluvatar. Mortal and numerous, Men are the dominant people of Middle-earth by the end of the Third Age. The Dunedain (descendants of Numenor) are the most noble lineage.

### Hobbits
Small, peaceful folk who love comfort, good food, and quiet lives. They live primarily in [[the-shire]] and are overlooked by the great powers, yet play a crucial role in the defeat of [[sauron]] through the actions of [[bilbo-baggins]], [[frodo-baggins]], and [[samwise-gamgee]].

### Dwarves
A hardy, secretive people skilled in mining and craftsmanship. They dwell in mountain strongholds and have a complex relationship with both Elves and Men.

### Ents
Ancient tree-like beings who are the shepherds of the forests. Led by Treebeard, they played a crucial role in the War of the Ring by destroying [[isengard]].

## The Ages of Middle-earth

- **First Age** — The age of the Silmarils, the wars of Morgoth (Sauron's master), and the great Elven kingdoms.
- **Second Age** — The rise and fall of Numenor, the forging of the [[rings-of-power]], and [[sauron]]'s first defeat.
- **Third Age** — The age of the [[the-one-ring]]'s discovery and destruction, culminating in the War of the Ring.
- **Fourth Age** — The Age of Men, beginning with the coronation of Aragorn as King of [[gondor]].
