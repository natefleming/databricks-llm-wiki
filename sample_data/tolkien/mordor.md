# Mordor

Mordor ("Black Land" in Sindarin) is the realm of [[sauron]] in the southeast of [[middle-earth]], serving as the primary seat of evil during both the Second and Third Ages. It is the location of [[mount-doom]], where [[the-one-ring]] was both forged and destroyed.

## Geography

Mordor is a roughly rectangular land bounded by mountain ranges on three sides:
- **Ered Lithui** (Ash Mountains) to the north
- **Ephel Duath** (Mountains of Shadow) to the west and south
- The eastern border is less defined, fading into the arid lands of Rhun

### Key Locations
- **Barad-dur** — The Dark Tower, [[sauron]]'s fortress and seat of power. Built with the power of [[the-one-ring]], it collapsed when the Ring was destroyed.
- **[[mount-doom]]** (Orodruin) — The volcanic mountain where Sauron forged [[the-one-ring]]. The only place where the Ring could be unmade.
- **Cirith Ungol** — A pass through the Ephel Duath guarded by a tower and the spider Shelob. The route [[frodo-baggins]] and [[samwise-gamgee]] used to enter Mordor.
- **The Black Gate** (Morannon) — The primary entrance to Mordor from the northwest, a massive fortified gate where Aragorn led the diversionary attack during the climax of the War of the Ring.
- **Gorgoroth** — The vast plateau of ash and slag between Mount Doom and Barad-dur.
- **Nurn** — The more fertile southern region of Mordor, where slave-worked farms fed Sauron's armies.

## Military Power

At the height of his power during the War of the Ring, [[sauron]] commanded:
- **Orcs** — Vast armies of corrupted creatures serving as foot soldiers
- **The Nazgul** — Nine Ringwraiths, lords enslaved by the [[rings-of-power]], led by the Witch-king
- **Trolls** — Including the massive Olog-hai bred for the wars
- **Men** — The Easterlings and Haradrim, Men from the east and south allied with or subject to Sauron
- **Fell Beasts** — Flying creatures ridden by the Nazgul

## Fall

When [[the-one-ring]] was destroyed in [[mount-doom]], the consequences for Mordor were immediate and catastrophic. Barad-dur collapsed. The Nazgul were destroyed. [[sauron]]'s will, which held his vast armies together, vanished. The mountains convulsed, Mount Doom erupted violently, and the military forces of Mordor scattered in confusion and despair.

The destruction of Mordor as a power allowed [[gondor]] under King Aragorn to extend its peace across the region, and the lands of Nurn were given to the freed slaves who had worked there.
