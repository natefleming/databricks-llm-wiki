# Sauron

Sauron, the Dark Lord, is the primary antagonist of The Lord of the Rings and the second Dark Lord of [[middle-earth]]. Originally a Maia of great skill, he was corrupted by the first Dark Lord, Morgoth, and became his most powerful servant before rising to become a Dark Lord in his own right.

## Origins

Sauron began as Mairon ("the Admirable"), a Maia associated with Aule the Smith, the Vala of craftsmanship. His innate love of order and perfection made him susceptible to Morgoth's promises of power and control. He became Morgoth's chief lieutenant during the First Age, commanding his fortresses and armies.

After Morgoth's defeat at the end of the First Age, Sauron initially considered repenting but refused to humble himself before the Valar. Instead, he went into hiding and eventually re-emerged as a new power in [[middle-earth]].

## The Rings of Power

### Deception of the Elves
In the Second Age, Sauron assumed a fair form and called himself Annatar ("Lord of Gifts"). He approached the Elven-smiths of Eregion and taught them the art of forging the [[rings-of-power]]. Together they created the Rings for Men and Dwarves, while the Elves crafted the Three Rings separately.

### Forging the One Ring
Sauron then secretly forged [[the-one-ring]] in the fires of [[mount-doom]], pouring much of his own power into it. The Ring was designed to control all other Rings of Power and dominate their bearers. When Sauron put on the One Ring, the Elves immediately perceived his treachery and removed their Three Rings.

### The Nine and the Seven
The Nine Rings given to Men turned their bearers into the Nazgul (Ringwraiths), undead servants completely enslaved to Sauron's will. The Seven Rings given to the Dwarves proved less effective — the Dwarves' stubborn nature resisted domination, though the Rings amplified their greed.

## Rise and Fall

### The Second Age
Sauron built [[mordor]] into a vast military empire and waged war across [[middle-earth]]. He was defeated by the Last Alliance of Elves and Men at the Battle of Dagorlad, and [[the-one-ring]] was cut from his hand by Isildur. Without the Ring, Sauron's spirit fled and he spent centuries formless and powerless.

### The Third Age
Sauron slowly rebuilt his power, first appearing as "the Necromancer" in Dol Guldur in Mirkwood (where [[gandalf]] first encountered signs of his return). Eventually he returned openly to [[mordor]], rebuilt the Dark Tower of Barad-dur, and began searching for [[the-one-ring]].

His strategy during the War of the Ring was focused on recovering the Ring and conquering [[gondor]]. He never imagined his enemies would try to destroy the Ring rather than wield it. When [[frodo-baggins]] carried the Ring into [[mount-doom]] and [[gollum]] inadvertently destroyed it, Sauron was utterly and permanently defeated — the power he had invested in the Ring was lost, and his spirit dissipated, never to take form again.

## Nature and Power

Sauron's defining trait is his desire for order through domination. Unlike Morgoth, who sought destruction for its own sake, Sauron wanted to organize and control all things according to his will. His greatest weapon was deception — he was a master of disguise, persuasion, and manipulation long before he resorted to military force.

His connection to [[the-one-ring]] was both his greatest strength and his fatal vulnerability. The Ring amplified his power enormously but also meant his fate was tied to a physical object that could be found and destroyed.
