# The Shire

The Shire is the homeland of the Hobbits in the northwest of [[middle-earth]], a peaceful agrarian region that serves as the starting and ending point of both The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings. Despite its seeming insignificance to the great powers of [[middle-earth]], the Shire produced the individuals who ultimately defeated [[sauron]].

## Geography and Settlement

The Shire is located in the region of Eriador, west of the Brandywine River. It is divided into four farthings (North, South, East, and West) and the additional regions of Buckland (east of the Brandywine) and the Westmarch.

Key settlements include:
- **Hobbiton** — Location of Bag End, home of [[bilbo-baggins]] and later [[frodo-baggins]]
- **Bywater** — A village near Hobbiton, site of the Battle of Bywater during the Scouring
- **Michel Delving** — The nominal capital, seat of the Mayor
- **Bucklebury** — Chief town of Buckland, home of the Brandybuck family

Hobbits live in hobbit-holes — comfortable underground dwellings built into hillsides, with round doors and windows, well-furnished interiors, and extensive gardens.

## Culture

Hobbits value comfort, routine, good food (six meals a day when possible), pipe-weed, gardening, genealogy, and gift-giving. They are deeply suspicious of adventures, outsiders, and anything that disrupts the comfortable order of Shire life.

Despite this insularity, Hobbits possess remarkable qualities that prove crucial in the wider world: extraordinary resilience, surprising courage when pressed, resistance to corruption (as demonstrated by both [[frodo-baggins]] and [[samwise-gamgee]] in bearing [[the-one-ring]]), and a groundedness that powerful beings like [[gandalf]] and Galadriel recognize as unique.

## Role in the War of the Ring

The Shire's isolation was partly maintained by the Rangers of the North (Aragorn's people), who protected its borders without the Hobbits' knowledge. [[gandalf]] recognized that the Hobbits' unassuming nature made them ideal Ring-bearers — [[sauron]] would never expect such insignificant creatures to carry his greatest weapon.

Four Hobbits of the Shire — [[frodo-baggins]], [[samwise-gamgee]], Merry Brandybuck, and Pippin Took — joined the Fellowship of the Ring and played decisive roles in the War of the Ring. Their success vindicated [[gandalf]]'s long faith in the "little people" of [[middle-earth]].

## The Scouring of the Shire

Upon returning from the War, the four Hobbits found the Shire occupied by ruffians under the direction of [[saruman]] (who had taken refuge there after the destruction of [[isengard]]). The returning heroes rallied the Shire-folk and expelled the invaders in the Battle of Bywater, the last battle of the War of the Ring.

[[samwise-gamgee]] led the restoration of the Shire, using the gift of earth from Galadriel to replant the trees that had been cut down. The year that followed (1420 Shire Reckoning) was remembered as the most beautiful and bountiful year in the Shire's history.
