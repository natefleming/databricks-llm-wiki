# Gandalf

Gandalf, known by many names across [[middle-earth]], is one of the five Istari (wizards) sent to Middle-earth by the Valar to aid the free peoples against the growing threat of [[sauron]]. He is one of the central characters in both The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings.

## Names and Titles

Gandalf is known by different names in different cultures:
- **Gandalf** — his name among the Men of the North and [[the-shire]]'s Hobbits
- **Mithrandir** — "Grey Pilgrim" in Sindarin, used by the Elves
- **Olorin** — his original name in Valinor before being sent to [[middle-earth]]
- **Tharkun** — his name among the Dwarves
- **Gandalf the Grey** — his title before his transformation
- **Gandalf the White** — his title after his resurrection and return

## Origins

Gandalf is a Maia, an angelic being who existed before the creation of the world. In Valinor, he was known as Olorin and was said to be the wisest of the Maiar. Around the year 1000 of the Third Age, he was sent to [[middle-earth]] along with four other Istari — [[saruman]], Radagast, and the two Blue Wizards — to oppose [[sauron]].

Unlike [[saruman]], who sought power directly, Gandalf worked through inspiration, counsel, and direct intervention at critical moments. He was given the Elven Ring Narya, the Ring of Fire, by Cirdan the Shipwright upon his arrival, which aided him in kindling hope and courage.

## Role in Key Events

### The Quest of Erebor
Gandalf orchestrated the quest to reclaim the Lonely Mountain from the dragon Smaug, bringing together [[bilbo-baggins]] and the company of Thorin Oakenshield. This seemingly minor adventure led to Bilbo's discovery of [[the-one-ring]], an event of enormous consequence.

### The Council of Elrond
At [[rivendell]], Gandalf played a central role in the Council of Elrond, where the decision was made to destroy [[the-one-ring]]. He had spent years researching the Ring's identity, confirming that Bilbo's ring was indeed Sauron's master weapon.

### Moria and the Balrog
In [[moria]], Gandalf confronted the Balrog of Morgoth on the Bridge of Khazad-dum, falling into the abyss with the creature. Their battle continued from the deepest dungeons to the peak of Zirak-zigil, ending with the Balrog's defeat and Gandalf's own death.

### Resurrection
Gandalf was sent back by the Valar as Gandalf the White, replacing [[saruman]] as the head of the Istari order. He returned with greater power and clarity of purpose.

### The War of the Ring
As Gandalf the White, he played decisive roles in the Battle of Helm's Deep, the liberation of [[isengard]], the defense of [[gondor]] at the Battle of the Pelennor Fields, and the final stand at the Black Gate of [[mordor]]. His strategy of sending [[frodo-baggins]] and [[samwise-gamgee]] to destroy [[the-one-ring]] while distracting Sauron's forces proved the key to victory.

## Character

Gandalf's defining traits include his deep wisdom, fierce compassion, occasional short temper, love of Hobbits and their simple pleasures, and his fondness for [[the-shire]]'s pipe-weed and fireworks. Despite his immense power, he consistently chose to empower others rather than dominate, embodying the principle that true strength lies in inspiring courage in those who have none.
