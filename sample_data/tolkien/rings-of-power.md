# The Rings of Power

The Rings of Power are twenty magical rings forged during the Second Age of [[middle-earth]], central to the events of The Lord of the Rings. They were created through a collaboration between [[sauron]] (disguised as Annatar) and the Elven-smiths of Eregion, led by Celebrimbor.

## The Twenty Rings

### The Three Elven Rings
The Three were forged by Celebrimbor alone, without [[sauron]]'s direct involvement, and are therefore free of his taint — though still subject to [[the-one-ring]]'s dominion. They are rings of preservation, healing, and protection:

- **Narya** (the Ring of Fire) — Set with a red stone. Originally held by Cirdan the Shipwright, who gave it to [[gandalf]] upon his arrival in [[middle-earth]]. It kindles hope and courage.
- **Nenya** (the Ring of Water) — Set with a white stone (adamant). Wielded by Galadriel of [[lothlorien]], it preserves and protects her realm.
- **Vilya** (the Ring of Air) — The mightiest of the Three, set with a blue stone. Wielded by Elrond at [[rivendell]], it provides healing and protection.

### The Seven Dwarf Rings
Given to the seven Dwarf-lords, these rings amplified the Dwarves' natural abilities — particularly their desire for treasure and craftsmanship. However, the Dwarves proved resistant to direct control, frustrating [[sauron]]'s plans. The rings did, however, kindle the Dwarves' greed, contributing to the great hoards that attracted dragons. By the end of the Third Age, four of the Seven had been consumed by dragon-fire and three had been recovered by Sauron.

### The Nine Rings of Men
Given to nine great lords of Men, these rings granted power, long life, and invisibility. Over time, however, each bearer faded into the wraith-world, becoming the Nazgul — the Ringwraiths, [[sauron]]'s most terrible servants. Led by the Witch-king of Angmar, the Nine are completely enslaved to the will of the Dark Lord through [[the-one-ring]].

### The One Ring
[[the-one-ring]] was forged by [[sauron]] alone in [[mount-doom]]. It is the master ring, designed to control all others. See the dedicated page for its full history.

## Purpose and Design

The Rings of Power were ostensibly created to preserve and heal — to slow the decay of the world that the Elves perceived and mourned. [[sauron]] exploited this desire, teaching ring-craft while secretly planning [[the-one-ring]] as a tool of domination.

The fundamental mechanic is hierarchical control: the One Ring governs the Three, the Seven, and the Nine. When [[sauron]] first put on the One Ring, the Elves immediately sensed his intention and hid their Three Rings. This began the war between Sauron and the Elves that shaped the Second Age.

## Destruction

When [[the-one-ring]] was destroyed in [[mount-doom]] at the end of the Third Age, the power of all the other Rings of Power faded. The Three Elven Rings lost their power to preserve and protect. [[lothlorien]] and [[rivendell]] began to diminish, and the Elves' time in [[middle-earth]] was truly over. This is why [[gandalf]], Galadriel, and Elrond departed for the Undying Lands after the War of the Ring — their work was done, and the world now belonged to Men.

## Themes

The Rings of Power embody Tolkien's exploration of the corrupting nature of power. Each ring offers its bearer something genuinely desirable — long life, preservation, mastery — but at the cost of freedom and ultimately identity. The paradox is that the power to control inevitably controls the one who wields it.
